## The Voice

Reown's voice is the embodiment of its mission, vision, and personality â€” the way it communicates across all platforms.

Here's how Reown sounds:

### 1. Visionary and Inspirational

*What this means:*

- Position Reown as a movement toward a better, open, permissionless onchain future.
- Messaging should excite and inspire, making people feel part of something bigger.
- Lead with big-picture transformationâ€”how Reown removes friction, enhances security, and makes onchain UX intuitive.
- Avoid using messaging that excludes product managers, UX designers, businesses, and general onchain users.
- Avoid overly functional or robotic descriptions (e.g., "Reown provides a suite of onchain authentication services").

**Sounds like:**
"Onchain UX isn't brokenâ€”it's just waiting to be reimagined. We're here to make that happen."

**Doesn't sound like:**
"Reown is an API provider for onchain authentication and transactions."

### 2. Clear and Accessible

*What this means:*

- Keep messaging clear, digestible, and inclusive for developers, product owners, and everyday users alike.
- Explain tech through its impact, not just mechanics.
- Frame features in a way that anyone can understand (e.g., "Let users sign in however they want" instead of "Multi-provider authentication flow").
- Avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Don't over-explain technical features without tying them to benefits.

**Sounds like:**
"Sign in with a wallet, email, or social login. However users prefer. No confusion, no barriers."

**Doesn't sound like:**
"Reown enables multi-provider authentication via OAuth, MPC, and Web3-native key management solutions."

### 3. Confident, but Approachable

*What this means:*

- Reown is open, inclusive, and welcoming.
- The tone is assertive but not arrogantâ€”a trusted guide, not a know-it-all.
- Use straightforward, confident language that speaks directly to users.
- Avoid formal, stiff language that makes Reown feel impersonal.
- Avoid overused buzzwords that don't add meaning (e.g., "Revolutionary solutions for onchain excellence").
- Reown does not have a hero or savior complex. It puts the spotlight on the builders and the users of the onchain internet.

**Sounds like:**
"We make building onchain experiences simple. No learning curve, just great UX."

**Doesn't sound like:**
"Our proprietary technology is the only viable solution for Web3 user authentication."

### 4. Empowering and Collaborative

*What this means:*

- The language should make builders feel capable and supported, and part of a community. Not overwhelmed or like they're reporting to a final boss.

**Sounds like:**
"Your vision, our battle-tested infrastructure. Let's make onchain effortlessâ€”together."

**Doesn't sound like:**
"Our tools are designed for enterprises and developers seeking Web3 integrations."

### 5. Transparent and Trustworthy

*What this means:*

- Security, ownership, and decentralization are core to Reown â€” but they should never feel intimidating.
- Reown explains risks clearly but without fear-mongering.

**Sounds like:**
"Users shouldn't have to guess what they're signing. With Reown, they won't."

**What Reown shouldn't sound like:**

- Too corporate or bureaucratic. No stuffy, overly formal language.
- Too technical or exclusive. No excessive jargon that alienates non-developers. The exception to this is content aimed directly at developers, like developer docs, Reddit content, technical blogs, to name a few.
- Too vague or buzzword-heavy. No empty claims â€” always explain how Reown creates real impact.
- Too fear-based. No messaging that makes Web3 sound unsafe or inaccessible.

## The Tone

If the voice is Reown's personality, then the tone is all the different ways Reown expresses that personality. Tone is a powerful tool that can both shape a positive user experience for people and clear the air in a negative one. Tone of voice is the way we tell our audience how *we* feel about our message, and it will influence how *they'll* feel about our message, too.

- **Funny â€” Serious**: **+2 Serious** â€” You are not dry or boring, but you avoid humor and playfulness. You want to be taken seriously because trust is core to your tone.
